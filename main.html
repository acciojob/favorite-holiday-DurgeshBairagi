Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city on the island of Honshu. Known for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses, Kyoto is a city that beautifully blends ancient history with modern culture.

  1. Kinkaku-ji - The Golden Pavilion, a stunning temple covered in gold leaf, surrounded by tranquil gardens.
  2. Fushimi Inari Shrine - Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates, leading up the sacred Mount Inari.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - A magical path through towering bamboo stalks, offering a serene experience.
  4. Kiyomizu-dera - An iconic temple with a large wooden terrace offering breathtaking views over Kyoto.
  5. Gion District - The heart of traditional Kyoto, where you can still see geishas and enjoy historic tea houses.
  • Kaiseki - A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner that showcases seasonal ingredients and exquisite presentation.
  • Yudofu - A simple yet flavorful dish made of tofu simmered in a light broth, often enjoyed in Kyoto's many temples.
  • Yuba - Tofu skin, a delicacy in Kyoto, served fresh or dried, and often used in various dishes.
  • Sushi - While not unique to Kyoto, the city's sushi is renowned for its fresh and high-quality ingredients.
  • Matcha Sweets - Kyoto is famous for its matcha (green tea) confections, from cakes to ice cream.
